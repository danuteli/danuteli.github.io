  • art station DUBULTI

    The only modern art space in Europe that is located in a functioning railway station

    A functional railway station in Dubulti has existed as early as 1877 however the current Modernist style built in concrete building resembling a breaking wave was only completed a whole century later in 1977. Designed by the Soviet architect Igors Javeins (1903—1980) Dubulti railway station served as the most modern station building in the Latvian Soviet Socialist Republic. Now an excellent example of combining transport, culture and public administration resources for making a unique platform of art related exhibitions accessible to everyone.

  • historical center of KRIMULDA

    The feeling of a small and peaceful village in the territory of the manor and its historical outbuildings. Surrounded by nature with a view over the valley of the river - silence, peace and connection with history

    Sitting on the brink of the ancient Gauja valley is the very heart of Krimulda's historical centre. One of the most notable examples of a classic villa to be found in the whole of Latvia. Built in 1822 by Prince Johan George Liven the Neoclassical cube-like structure decorated by a massive porch has witnesst two centuries worth of history including the latter almost half-century until today as being a certified rehabilitation centre. The scenic view of the primeval valley from the precipice terrace, the balcony, or the little tower makes for a truly breath-taking experience that can only further be enhanced by a journey in the cable car linking both banks of the Gauja river. History is literary to be found on every corner in Krimulda as for example the stable, built-in 1893, serves as an excellent example of traditional asymmetric style construction using broken boulders. Also on the estate, you can view the steward’s house, a barn, an oasthouse, and Swiss house - one of the last remaining examples of the boarding-house type buildings. They make one clearly imagine the former life that was led here. "Almost as many as there are berries and flowers to be found in Latvia there are equal amounts of diffeent wines". Such is the saying of local winemaker Jānis Mikans. Krimulda's manor's winery is rich for producing and evergrowing list of different varieties of wines, liqueurs, a type of calvados and also gin created from locally grown produce and offering the unique chance of tasting these beverages shall provide further learning about local history and traditions. The 13th century medieval castle once schorched by fire with only ruins remaining to tell of its former glory still offers a view worthy of the name it is often given "Bellevue" meaning "beautiful view" in French.

  • fishing boat graveyard in MAZIRBE

    Fishing boats and their parts that were laid out on the Mazirbe coast in 1960s, are now gradually surrounded by forest

    There is no small number of fishing boats that were forcefully laid to rest in many seaside villages during the 60's Soviet ban of individual fishing and Mazirbe's boat graveyard serves as one such location were it is still evident today as nature slowly reclaims that what remains of the old ship wrecks. Until 2nd World War there were around 48 boats in the graveyard now reduced to wrecks of eight to tell their tale.

  • former military territory KARAOSTA

    As a closed military territory during the Soviet era, this part of the city of Liepaja currently stands out from the rest of the city with its harshness and unique functionality in architecture and objects built for military purposes

    In 1890. within the nothern part of the city of Liepāja by the order of then Tsar of Russia Alexander III a completely autonomous extensive fortress and military city was constructed. Originally named Port of the Emperor Alexander III now known simply as Karosta (War Port) for it is in fact the largest historical military garrison in the Baltics constituting almost a third of Liepaja's territory Karosta holds many unique locales of great historical value within its boundaries. One of such places is the only military prison in Europe open to tourists named Karosta Prison. Its inmates at the time were revolutionaries, sailors of the Tsar's Navy and non-commissioned officers, German deserters, 'enemies of the state' during the Stalin's rule soldiers of the Soviet army and the Latvian army. Now recognized as the most unusual and surprising hotel in the world. Built from 1903. to 1904. The Karosta Manege facility could accommodate more than whopping 4000 people and thus was used for physical training exercises of sailors as for official receptions and honorary dinners for garrison sailors. Though time has not spared this historic building and only the walls of the Manege remain the former grandeur of the building can still be felt even today. The stately red towering building is an outstanding example of industrial architecture and an integral part of Karosta’s scenery. In 2010, the Liepāja Karosta water tower was granted the status of an industrial cultural monument of local significance. During summer season the tower serves the noble purpose of holding open workshops of artists for people to experience many different creative processes and as a housing for many exhibitions. The longest breakwater in Latvia - the Northern Breakwater - was built at the end of the 19th century as a crucial part of Liepaja's fortress and military port. A large parking lot by the Northern Breakwater aserves as a great place to park your car. There are also lavatory facilities for visitors to fully enjoy the presence of this historic locaton. Further up Karosta's northern shore lies the the impressive Fortification battery No. 1. or rather what is left standing after it was oficially demolished on November 1908. Commonly refered to as The Northern Fort only partially blown up parts of the artillery batteries and underground structures are still standing today signaling of its past glory and magnitude.
